Biography

Tracy Lamar McGrady Jr. (born May 24, 1979) is an American former professional basketball player, best known for his career in the National Basketball Association (NBA). McGrady is a seven-time NBA All-Star, seven-time All-NBA selection, two-time NBA scoring champion, and winner of the NBA Most Improved Player Award in 2001.

Tracy McGrady was selected as the ninth overall pick by the Toronto Raptors in the 1997 NBA draft. Then he went to the Orlando Magic where he was voted the league's Most Improved Player in 2000. Undoubtedly at that time it was his prime for his career.

Tracy was traded to Houston Rockets in 2004. The Rockets struggled to begin the 2004–05 season, posting a losing record of 16–17 through their first 33 games.[35] In December, McGrady had one of the most memorable performances of his career, scoring 13 points in the final 35 seconds against the San Antonio Spurs to secure a comeback victory. The sequence included four consecutive three-pointers, one of which was part of a four-point play and the last of which was a game-winner in the final two seconds.

However, during 2005-2010, Tracy had missed more than 50 games because of back spasms, left shoulder and knee injuries. Despite the fact that he suffered from these horrible injuries pain, in 2007-2008, In 2007–08, the Rockets went on a 22-game winning streak, which was the then-second longest in NBA history, despite missing Yao Ming during that stretch.[46] During their run, McGrady expressed optimism over the team's roster, admitting, "I haven't had this kind of trust in my teammates before."

For the rest of his career, Tracy had traded to Detroit Pistons, Atlanta Hawks,Qingdao Eagles (team at CBA) and San Antonio Spurs. On On August 26, McGrady officially announced his retirement from the NBA.

On 2017, Tracy was inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame.
